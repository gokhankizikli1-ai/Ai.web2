# DECISIONS.md

# KorvixAI Architecture Decision Records (ADR)

Version: 1.0

Last Updated: June 2026

---

# Purpose

This document records important architectural and engineering decisions made throughout the development of KorvixAI.

The purpose is to preserve the reasoning behind major technical choices so future contributors understand **why** a decision was made, not only **what** was implemented.

Every major architectural decision should be recorded here.

---

# ADR-001

## Title

KorvixAI is an AI Operating System

## Status

Accepted

## Date

June 2026

## Decision

KorvixAI will not be developed as another chatbot.

Instead, it will become a complete AI Operating System capable of managing software engineering, businesses, research, trading, automation, and multi-agent collaboration.

## Reason

The future of AI is autonomous execution rather than conversational interfaces alone.

Building an AI Operating System creates a long-term competitive advantage.

---

# ADR-002

## Title

Multi-Agent Architecture

## Status

Accepted

## Date

June 2026

## Decision

Complex tasks should eventually be executed by multiple specialized AI agents working together instead of a single assistant.

## Reason

Specialized agents improve quality, scalability, parallel execution, and maintainability.

Future capabilities such as software engineering, Shopify creation, research, trading, and automation naturally benefit from collaborative agents.

---

# ADR-003

## Title

Workflow Engine Based Execution

## Status

Accepted

## Date

June 2026

## Decision

KorvixAI will execute complex tasks through a Workflow Engine using Directed Acyclic Graphs (DAGs).

## Reason

DAG execution supports dependency management, retry logic, parallel execution, monitoring, and future distributed workloads.

---

# ADR-004

## Title

Security First Architecture

## Status

Accepted

## Date

June 2026

## Decision

Authentication, authorization, user isolation, and secure session management are mandatory foundations.

Security improvements always take priority over new features.

## Reason

User trust is fundamental.

A feature-rich platform without strong security is not acceptable.

---

# ADR-005

## Title

Documentation-Driven Development

## Status

Accepted

## Date

June 2026

## Decision

Documentation is considered part of the implementation.

A feature is not complete until the relevant documentation has been updated.

## Reason

As KorvixAI grows, documentation becomes essential for maintainability, onboarding, AI context, and long-term development.

---

# ADR-006

## Title

Production-First Engineering

## Status

Accepted

## Date

June 2026

## Decision

Every implementation should be production-ready from the beginning.

Temporary hacks, hidden shortcuts, and experimental production code should be avoided.

## Reason

Maintaining high engineering standards reduces future technical debt and increases reliability.

---

# ADR-007

## Title

Modular Service Architecture

## Status

Accepted

## Date

June 2026

## Decision

Each subsystem should remain modular, independent, and replaceable.

Examples include:

* Authentication
* Chat
* Workflow Engine
* Agent Runtime
* Project Service
* Memory
* Trading
* Research

## Reason

Loose coupling improves scalability, testing, maintenance, and future expansion.

---

# ADR-008

## Title

AI Provider Abstraction

## Status

Accepted

## Date

June 2026

## Decision

Large Language Models should always be accessed through a routing layer rather than directly.

Supported providers include:

* OpenAI
* Anthropic Claude
* Google Gemini

Additional providers should integrate through the same abstraction layer.

## Reason

Provider abstraction improves flexibility, reliability, and future model selection.
